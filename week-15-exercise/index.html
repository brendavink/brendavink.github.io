FrameWork

Tapping into the existing knowledge, skills, and experience of non-traditional students and transfering these skills to first year students, providing the best college experience possible.

Learn more about FrameWork

Mentoring is a unique method of supporting students to improve their learning and leadership skills, motivating them towards their future career development.

Why mentor?

Mentoring creates a more rewarding one-on-one experience for students, this includes freshmen and the non-traditional studemts.

Benefits

Mentorship is to tap into the existing knowledge, skills, and experience of the more seasoned students and transfer these skills to freshmen students.

How it works

The program/event will pair freshmen students with non traditional student to guide them through their first year of college.

More events:

The program not only assists new students but it also gives the non traditional students a way to feel included. Throughout the year there will be a number of events for all mentors and their students to participate in.

Fashion show

A fun way to raise money for charity and also walk the runway in your coolest attire.

Cooking class

Cooking classes help to keep students healthy and prepare them to start being more self-sufficient.

Paint jam

Grab some brushes, paint, and a canvas, and you’ve got yourself a paint jam.
